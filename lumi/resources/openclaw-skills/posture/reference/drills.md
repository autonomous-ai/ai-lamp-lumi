# Per-offender drills

When the route resolves to **L5** (high risk OR repeated medium), the coaching
line should include a *concrete, 5-second action* the user can do without
leaving their chair. Pick from the bank below — paraphrase, never quote.

Each region has 2-3 drill options. **Vary** across nudges in the same session
so the user doesn't hear the same drill twice in a row. Pair the drill with
the observation line from `phrasing.md`.

A good L5 reads like: `<observation> + <drill> [+ optional why]`. Adapt the
description into the user's language at runtime — the bank uses English to
keep the skill file portable; the spoken output should be whatever language
the user is using.

## Drill bank

### `neck_flexed` (forward flexion)

- **Chin tuck** — pull chin straight back, push the crown of the head up, hold 5 sec
- **Look-up + breathe** — raise head until eyes are level with the screen, take 2 deep breaths
- **Shoulder release + spine lengthen** — exhale, drop shoulders away from ears, lengthen the spine

### `neck_extended` (backward extension)

- **Lower the eye line** — drop the monitor or move the chair closer so the head sits level
- **Chin-to-chest 5s** — gently tuck the chin down, rest the cervical extensors

### `neck_twisted` (rotation / side-bend)

- **Rotate the whole chair** — turn the chair instead of twisting the waist
- **Re-square the head** — both ears level, aligned over the shoulders
- **Ear-to-shoulder 10s** — gently tilt the ear toward the opposite shoulder for a stretch release

### `trunk_flexed` (forward bend)

- **Lean back into the chair** — slide the chair in, plant the lower back against the lumbar support
- **Hip angle ~90°** — check the hip angle, both feet flat on the floor
- **Stand up for 10s** — stand and reach overhead twice

### `trunk_bent_low` (deep flexion)

- **Stand up, three steps** — too deep to correct seated; the user needs to break the trunk position
- **Reset the chair** — sit back down with hips deep in the chair, spine stacked

### `trunk_twisted` (rotation)

- **Rotate the chair** — don't twist at the waist
- **Square shoulders over hips** — check that both shoulders sit at the same height

### `upper_arm_flexed` (elbow raised)

- **Pull keyboard closer** — keep the elbow loose at the side
- **Drop the elbow** — relax the shoulder, let the elbow fall toward the hip

### `upper_arm_raised` (arm high)

- **Drop the shoulders** — exhale, let the shoulders settle away from the ears
- **Hand down to the desk** — rest the hand flat on the desk, not floating

### `upper_arm_extended` (arm behind body)

- **Pull the hand forward** — bring the hand back in front of the body
- **Use the armrest** — rest the elbow on the chair's armrest if one exists

### `lower_arm_strained` (forearm angle off)

- **Elbow ~90°** — adjust chair or keyboard height for a right-angle elbow
- **Forearm stretch 5s** — extend the arm forward, gently rotate the wrist

### `wrist_deviated` (ulnar/radial deviation)

- **Place mouse in front of the shoulder** — don't park it far out at the hip
- **Align with the forearm** — the wrist should continue the line of the forearm

### `wrist_strained` (flexion/extension)

- **Add a wrist rest** — use a gel pad or rolled towel under the wrist
- **Lower the chair one notch** — let the wrist sit flat over the keys

## Mixing rules

- **Pair the drill with the region you named in the observation.** Don't open
  with "your neck is flexed" and then suggest a wrist drill — the listener
  loses the thread.
- **One drill per nudge.** Even if 2 regions trigger, suggest the drill for
  the dominant region. Stacking two drills in one breath dilutes both.
- **Skip the drill on L4** (one-liner). L4 is just observation + name
  ("Neck.", "Right arm high."). Drills belong to L5.
- **Never repeat the exact same drill phrase** in consecutive L5 nudges. The
  bank gives 2-3 options per region — rotate them.

## Cross-skill bonus

When the same turn fires the cross-skill condition (posture + sedentary >60
min from `[wellbeing_context]`), the *break itself* is the drill. Don't list
a separate posture drill — frame standing up as the fix:

*Tone, paraphrased:* "Stand up and walk a few steps — you'll straighten the
back and rest the eyes at the same time, two birds with one stone."
